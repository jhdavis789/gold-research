Gold · Capital Market Assumptions

A top-down valuation framework for gold

Gold's price is a chain of shares: a slice of the world's hedge demand, which is a slice of the world's wealth — divided by a slowly growing pile of ounces. Written multiplicatively it's a funnel; written in logs it's an additive expected-return identity, which is exactly what a CMA needs to output.

★ The World's Gold Holders — share of reserves & tonnes of gold by top country, over time →

P  =  ( W × s₁ × s₂ )  ÷  Q
∂ ln P  =  nominal wealth growth  +  Δ hedge share  +  Δ gold-of-hedges share  −  supply growth
Two reference cases fall out cleanly. Share-constant: hold both share terms flat — gold keeps its current slice — and E[return] = (population + productivity + inflation) − ~1.6% supply ≈ ~3–4% nominal / +0.5–1% real. But "flat" is not neutral — it holds gold at its 97th-percentile share (see anchors below). The honest mean-reversion neutral lets Δln(s₁·s₂) run negative toward the historical median, pulling the base case below that. Your active thesis (debasement, distrust, real rates, war) is the gap between the two — a scenario overlay, not a free-handed target.
Frame — what this can and can't do

It's a stock identity, so a share is an outcome, not a driver

P = W·s₁·s₂/Q is an accounting identity — it gives the price consistent with a set of shares, but doesn't itself say why the shares move. The engine underneath is the marginal flow: net new official + investment demand clearing against a near-fixed ~1.6%/yr supply flow. Treat the funnel as scaffolding for a scenario, and the holder table below as the flow mechanism that fills it in. (Numerator G and denominator Q are both total above-ground gold, so G/Q = spot exactly — no monetary-vs-jewellery mismatch.)

Caveats

Two things to keep honest

Pushback — total wealth to banks

Central banks don't scale off global wealth

You floated "total wealth" as the central-bank base — I'd push back. A reserve manager's gold demand scales off the size and USD-concentration of its own FX reserve pile (~$12–13T globally), which is driven by trade and current-account surpluses, not aggregate world wealth. Total wealth is the right scaler for households and investors, who hold gold as a share of personal/portfolio wealth. So H really has two sub-bases: a wealth-scaled retail+investor channel, and a reserves-scaled official channel. Post-2022 the official channel has been the marginal price-setter — which is why your static real-rate fit broke.

Caution — don't double-count across layers

Three variables touch two layers each — partition them

Inflation: it lifts all nominal assets via W, so s₂'s "debasement" must be the excess beyond CPI (the regime premium — proxy with CB net buying / real-rate gap / lease rates), or you lift gold with inflation twice. Real rates: the s₁ "financial repression" lever is the real cash rate (DTB3); s₂'s opportunity-cost leg is the 10y real rate (DFII10) — keep them as the two distinct factors your model already separates, don't apply one shock twice. Sanctions: raise overall hedge demand (s₁) and shift gold's share within reserves (s₂) — apply once per layer with separate elasticities. Calibrate the s₂ rate leg to your factor-model betas, not by hand.
