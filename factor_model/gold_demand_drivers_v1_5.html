5 · More ways to see it

event study · directional scatters · forward returns by flow bucket · rolling lead/lag

Event study. Avg weekly ETF flow in the ±6 weeks around the biggest gold up-weeks (top decile, n=113) and down-weeks (n=110). Flow peaks at the move (k=0) and stays elevated after — coincident-to-lagging, not anticipatory.

Rolling lead/lag strength. 104-wk correlation of flow with the prior 4-wk return. Persistently positive — the relationship is a stable feature, not a one-off.

R² = 0.0929 ; slope = 1.1628 , p = 1.84e-25

R² = 0.0006 ; slope = 0.0064 , p = 0.4134

Forward 4-wk gold return by ETF-flow quintile. There is an upward gradient (Q1 0.34% → Q4 1.29%), and it survives controlling for the prior-4-wk return — it actually strengthens (per-quintile slope 0.25→0.37), so it is not just momentum bleed-through. The genuine caution is in the system test: the flows→future-returns direction is only marginally significant (Granger p=0.066) versus the strongly significant past-return→flow link (p<0.001), and it is HAC/spec-dependent. So treat this gradient as suggestive, not established out-of-sample predictive power.

6 · Other analyses

holdings vs price · the real-rate decoupling

Global ETF holdings (tonnes) vs gold price. Holdings rose into 2012, bled out 2013–15 (taper), surged in 2020, and—tellingly—gold made new highs in 2024–25 while ETF holdings lagged: the recent leg is central-bank/physical-led, not ETF-led.

Rolling 104-wk beta of weekly gold return to Δ real rate. Persistently negative (higher real rates → lower gold), but the magnitude has drifted toward zero post-2022 — the much-discussed gold/real-rate "decoupling" as central-bank buying took over. (Stronger at quarterly frequency; see regimes below.)

The suggested regime shift is in central banks: CB β flips sign pre→post-2022 (−0.016 → +0.017) — but both periods are individually insignificant (p=0.27, p=0.28) and the difference is only marginal (interaction p=0.20; n=17 post-2022). Read it as a plausible shift, not statistically confirmed. What is robust: CB demand roughly doubled (≈118→254 t/qtr), and ETF flow's behaviour (lagging, not leading) is stable across both regimes.
